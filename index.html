About

My work sits at the intersection of energy economics and development policy. Trained in both economics and environmental science at Seoul National University, I focus on how developing economies can finance and navigate the energy transition — and who bears its costs.

I have estimated Korea's social cost of carbon with integrated assessment models, analyzed the total cost of ownership of electric motorcycles in Kenya under high interest rates, and co-founded a nonprofit in Bangladesh that turned shrimp-shell waste into water remediation while employing local women.

I am preparing for doctoral study, where I hope to keep asking one question: how do we design climate and energy policy that works where it is needed most?
